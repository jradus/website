Don’t We All

I wrote this backward.
At least, wrote the last two lines first,
and worked from there.
This is not a joke,
but it is written like one.

I think the snakes in the post office
are a metaphor.
And I think the metaphor
is as subtle as dozens of snakes in a post office.

If I opened the door to a post office,
and was met with snakes,
I would simply close the door and never return.
I would probably tell somebody
“The post office is full of snakes.”
But there’s no guarantee they’re going to believe you,
even less guarantee they’ll do anything.

The post office was full of snakes,
but, really, what makes me think I’m so special.

Vaping In My Altima: A Lyric Poem About The Guy In Front Of Me At Taco Bell

The entire side of my face is wet.
I put the windows down in my car
because, while it’s gray and has been raining
it’s not too bad out right now.
I got splashed by a car entering
the work parking lot as I was leaving.
But my heart is set on garbage food,
and nothing can bring me down.

I pull in,
to the merchant that peddles
the instruments of my untimely death,
in 10-20 years.
There he is.
In an Altima,
the color of champagne,
though, it’s not really
the color of champagne,
but it’s a color that we’ve all agreed to call champagne,
at least, as that color is applied to cars.
Mostly, every accent
that was originally chrome,
is now black,
including the car emblems and the wheels.
There’s a small dent in the trunk,
probably from horseplay,
but maybe he backed into a tree.

And so, there we are,
in each other’s company,
desiring the same things,
living the same life
for 10-20 minutes.
Out of his cracked window
rolls a thick puff of white smoke.
Cotton.
Fat rigs, ohms, and cotton candy.
It’s unmistakable,
and maybe a little silly,
but in that moment he is happy,
and I am happy with him.

My burrito was a sloppy mess,
but the taco wasn’t bad.

Y2K

Thinking about how
one the last day of the
old millennium,
or perhaps the first day
of the new,
you were with your family
at the animal shelter,
scared of every single dog.

And in 7 years,
the dog you eventually adopted
would be dead,
and your family would be
ripping at the seams
while you were asleep in a dorm
half a state away.

And 7 years after,
you’d be losing yourself,
to a million little personal tragedies,
which seemed like
the end of the line at the time,
but that was,
not quite 7 years ago.

You’ve got 2 new families, now,
and 2 new dogs.
